## About This Handbook
This handbook explains how we work together at [Company Name] and what employees
can expect. If anything here is unclear or seems out of date, contact the HR team
at [hr@yourcompany.com].

## Working Hours
Standard hours are [9:00 AM to 5:30 PM], Monday to Friday, with a one-hour lunch.
Core collaboration hours, when everyone should be reachable, are [10:00 AM to 4:00 PM].
Flexible start and end times are fine as long as core hours are covered and your
manager agrees.

## Remote & Hybrid Work
Employees may work remotely up to [3] days per week. [Tuesdays and Thursdays] are
in-office days for everyone. Fully remote arrangements require [director] approval.

## Paid Time Off (PTO)
Full-time employees receive [20] days of paid vacation per year, accrued monthly.
Up to [5] unused days may be carried into the next year; anything beyond that is
forfeited. Request time off at least [2 weeks] in advance through your manager.

## Sick Leave
Employees receive [10] paid sick days per year, separate from PTO. For absences
longer than [3] consecutive days, a doctor's note is required. Notify your manager
as early as possible on the day of a sick absence.

## Public Holidays
The company observes [list your public holidays here]. When a public holiday falls
on a weekend, [describe your policy, e.g. the following Monday is given in lieu].

## Parental & Family Leave
Birthing parents receive [16] weeks of paid parental leave. Non-birthing parents
receive [8] weeks. Leave must be taken within [12 months] of the birth or adoption.
[Adjust to match your local legal requirements.]

## Bereavement Leave
Employees may take up to [5] days of paid bereavement leave for the loss of an
immediate family member, and [1] day for extended family or close friends.

## Health & Benefits
The company covers [100%] of the employee health insurance premium and [50%] for
dependents. Benefits begin on the [first day of the month after your start date].
Open enrollment is held each [November].

## Expenses & Reimbursement
Work-related expenses under [$100] can be approved by your manager. Anything over
[$100] requires [director] approval before the purchase. Submit receipts within
[30] days through [your expense process].

## Company Equipment & IT
The company provides the equipment you need to do your job. Keep it secure, use it
mainly for work, and report any loss, theft, or security concern to [IT contact]
immediately. Return all equipment when you leave.

## Code of Conduct
We expect everyone to treat colleagues, clients, and partners with respect and
honesty. Harassment, discrimination, bullying, and retaliation of any kind are not
tolerated.

## Equal Opportunity
[Company Name] is an equal-opportunity employer. We do not discriminate on the basis
of race, religion, gender, age, disability, sexual orientation, or any other
protected characteristic, in any employment decision.

## Confidentiality & Data
Employees must keep company and client information confidential, both during and
after employment. Handle client data only as permitted by our agreements and by law.

## Performance & Reviews
Formal performance reviews happen [twice a year]. Feedback is meant to be ongoing,
honest, and supportive — your manager is your first point of contact for goals and
growth.

## Raising a Concern
To raise a concern, report misconduct, or discuss a personal or sensitive matter,
contact the HR team at [hr@yourcompany.com] or speak with your manager. All reports
are handled confidentially, and retaliation for raising a good-faith concern is not
tolerated.

## Leaving the Company
Employees are asked to give [2 weeks'] written notice of resignation. On your last
day, return all company equipment and complete any handover with your manager.
